Scope of Services

From weld-on parts to complete structures.

Assemblies & single parts

From a single weld-on part to a complete welded structure: we join stainless steel at any scale — precisely to your drawing or from our own engineering.

Documented quality

Welding quality to DIN EN ISO 3834-3: on request with weld documentation, welding procedure specifications and inspection reports — up to certified pressure vessels to AD 2000.

Materials

Austenitic stainless steels such as 1.4301 (304), 1.4404 (316L) and 1.4571 (316Ti) are our daily business — welded with the process that suits the seam, not the other way around.

When you outsource stainless steel welding, you are buying trust: the weld determines tightness, corrosion resistance and the service life of the component. That is why Klarmann welds corrosion-resistant materials exclusively — with welding specialists who have done nothing else for decades, and a process chain certified to DIN EN ISO 3834-3. For pressure vessels, add our manufacturer qualifications to AD 2000 (HP 0 / HP 100R) and the ASME U-Stamp.

As a contract manufacturer we take on production welding for machine and plant builders as well as for companies from the pharmaceutical, chemical, cosmetics and food industries: individual weld-on parts, complete assemblies or series — to your drawing, with material certificates and full weld documentation on request. Grinding and pickling & passivation follow directly on site — your assembly leaves the plant ready to install.

FAQ

In brief.

What documentation do I receive for the welds? add

Depending on your requirements we provide material certificates, welding procedure specifications (WPS) and inspection reports — up to the complete documentation package for pressure vessels to AD 2000. Just tell us which code applies to your project.

Do you also take on pure welding jobs without further fabrication? add

Yes. As a contract manufacturer we also weld supplied parts to your drawing — from one-off pieces to series production. Cutting, forming, grinding and pickling can be added as needed.

Which materials do you weld? add

Our daily business is austenitic stainless steel such as 1.4301 (304), 1.4404 (316L) and 1.4571 (316Ti). We process corrosion-resistant alloys exclusively — so your weld stays free of ferrous contamination.

Do you build pressure vessels with third-party acceptance? add

Yes — design, fabrication and testing to AD 2000 are part of our core business, on request with acceptance by independent inspection bodies and to ASME U-Stamp for international projects.
