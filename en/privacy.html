Privacy Policy

Protecting your personal data matters to us. Below we explain which data is processed when you visit this website and use our forms, for which purposes, and which rights you have. The German version of this privacy policy is authoritative.

1. Controller

Klarmann Edelstahlverarbeitung GmbH
Willerfang 2
26655 Westerstede-Ocholt, Germany

Phone: +49 4409 9288-0
Email: info@klarmann-edelstahl.de
Represented by the managing director: Rolf Klarmann

For privacy matters please contact: datenschutz@klarmann-edelstahl.de

2. General information on data processing

We process personal data only to the extent necessary to provide a functional website and to handle your inquiries and applications. The legal bases are in particular Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR (consent), Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (contract and pre-contractual measures) and Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest). Data is only shared with third parties where one of these legal bases permits it — for example with the processors named below who provide hosting and inquiry management on our behalf.

3. Hosting and server log files

This website is hosted on Google Firebase Hosting, a service of Google Ireland Limited, Gordon House, Barrow Street, Dublin 4, Ireland. When you access the website, the hosting provider automatically processes connection data (server log files), in particular IP address, date and time of access, requested page, transferred data volume, browser type and operating system. This data is technically required to deliver the website and to ensure the security and stability of the system (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR); it is not merged with other data.

A data processing agreement pursuant to Art. 28 GDPR is in place with Google. Processing may also take place on servers in the USA; Google LLC is certified under the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework, which ensures an adequate level of data protection.

4. SSL/TLS encryption

This website uses SSL/TLS encryption. Data you submit to us via the website — such as inquiries or applications — is protected against third-party access during transmission. You can recognise the encrypted connection by "https://" and the lock symbol in your browser's address bar.

5. Contact form and inquiries

When you use our contact form, we process the details you enter (name, company, email address, phone number, requested service and your message) in order to handle your inquiry and possible follow-up questions. The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR where your inquiry is aimed at concluding a contract, and otherwise our legitimate interest in answering inquiries (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).

To receive and manage inquiries we use a lead management system operated by our marketing agency (available at mih-portal.de, hosted in a data centre of IONOS SE in Germany). The agency processes this data exclusively on our behalf and according to our instructions on the basis of a data processing agreement pursuant to Art. 28 GDPR. After submitting the form you will receive an automatic confirmation email. Your data is deleted as soon as it is no longer required for processing and no statutory retention obligations apply.

6. Applications

Via the application form on our careers page we process the data you provide (desired position, qualification, experience, availability, name, phone number and optionally email address) solely for the purpose of the application procedure. The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR in conjunction with Section 26 of the German Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG). Transmission and management take place via the system described in section 5, operated by our agency as processor; only persons involved in the recruitment process have access to application data.

Application documents are deleted no later than six months after completion of the application procedure, unless you are hired or have consented to longer storage (e.g. for future vacancies).

7. Contact by email or phone

If you contact us by email or phone, we process your details to handle the matter (Art. 6(1)(b) or (f) GDPR). The data is deleted once it is no longer required for this purpose and no retention obligations apply.

8. Cookies and local storage

This website does not use cookies or tracking technologies without your consent. Only your decision in the cookie notice itself — where displayed — is stored locally in your browser (localStorage) so that the prompt does not reappear on every visit. This storage is technically necessary (Section 25(2) of the German TDDDG). Services requiring consent (see section 9) are only loaded after your explicit approval; you can change or withdraw your selection at any time via the "Cookie settings" link in the footer.

9. Meta Pixel and Conversions API (advertising campaigns)

To measure the performance of our advertising on Facebook and Instagram we use — exclusively with your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR, Section 25(1) TDDDG) — the Meta Pixel and the Meta Conversions API, services of Meta Platforms Ireland Limited, Merrion Road, Dublin 4, Ireland ("Meta"). Event data (e.g. a visit to this website or the submission of a form) is transmitted to Meta together with technical identifiers in order to measure the effectiveness of our ads and to build audiences. Without your consent these services are not loaded and no data is transmitted to Meta.

For the collection and transmission of event data we are jointly responsible with Meta (Art. 26 GDPR); the corresponding agreement is available from Meta. Meta is solely responsible for the subsequent processing. Data may be transferred to Meta Platforms, Inc. in the USA; Meta is certified under the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework. Further information can be found in Meta's data policy. You can withdraw your consent at any time with effect for the future via the "Cookie settings" link in the footer.

10. Fonts and scripts

All fonts and script libraries used on this website are hosted locally. Apart from the hosting itself, no connections to third-party servers are established when you load a page, and no data is transmitted to font or CDN providers.

11. Google Maps (link only)

We do not embed a map on our contact page; we merely link to Google Maps. Only when you click the link do you leave our website, from which point Google's privacy terms apply. Simply using our website does not transmit any data to Google Maps.

12. Your rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access to the data processed (Art. 15 GDPR)
  • Rectification of inaccurate data (Art. 16 GDPR)
  • Erasure (Art. 17 GDPR)
  • Restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR)
  • Data portability (Art. 20 GDPR)
  • Objection to processing based on Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (Art. 21 GDPR)
  • Withdrawal of consent with effect for the future (Art. 7(3) GDPR)

To exercise your rights, an informal message to datenschutz@klarmann-edelstahl.de is sufficient. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority; the authority responsible for us is the State Commissioner for Data Protection of Lower Saxony, Prinzenstraße 5, 30159 Hanover, Germany.

13. Changes to this privacy policy

We update this privacy policy whenever the legal situation or the services used on this website change. The version published here applies.
